New article! Read about Gunman Chronicles, an officially sanctioned Half-Life mod that acts as a stepping stone between corridor shooters and the expansive scope of later titles like Halo.

Gunman Chronicles

The game opens in a collapsing, prehistoric jungle temple where the General’s stooges ambush your allies in a crossfire; the massive breadth and complexity of this scene dwarfs nearly anything attempted before it. The game doesn’t often evoke that sense of innovation, though, mainly because of its close resemblance to its forebears. The ingredients for a more daring shooter are in place, but it plays like… well, a Half-Life mod. A great Half-Life mod, of course, but one working on a larger canvas with the same paints. When you drive a tank across a desert or evade a drone ship patrolling the skies, those moments are exciting and fresh but still distant from the genre’s epic battles to come.

Nightwatch story: the ending

Sourced from tweets in August 2014 by Andrew Weldon, Nightwatch founder and project director:

…the ending of Pacific Rim is the planned ending of Nightwatch.

In the Nightwatch story, Xen was one of many “border worlds” that directly linked portals, hence Black Mesa portals arriving there.

The resonance cascade at Black Mesa turned Earth into a portal beacon, ripping life from Xen, and opening a door to anyone with portal tech.

This is why Race X from Op4 showed up, as well as scouts from a new enemy in Nightwatch, who had clashed with Black Ops forces in Xen.

In Nightwatch, Black Ops had established outposts in Xen to mine the then-intact planet core, eventually destabilizing and destroying it.

So, the Black Mesa survivors dodged the Op4 nuke by jumping to a Black Ops outpost in Xen, then sent a nuke through a portal to the new enemy’s world.

Then the player character and colleague Lt. Alexis Robinson were whisked away by the G-Man, who monologued about deus ex machinas…

…and sent you back to Earth to prepare for the war that was about to come.
